First of all the Palazzo is simply the boutique part of the actual Bauer hotel and it would seem that the differences between the two are minimal. From what I could gather they were:  The Bauer had an impressive reception area compared with the Palazzo's rather pokey little room.  The Bauer is accessed via a main square whereas the Palazzo is accessed by a narrow alleyway full of cooking smells.  The Bauer doesn't have any rooms with Grande Canal views whereas the Palazzo's rooms generally do.   The Bauer guests take their breakfast at the canal side in the De Pisis restaurant whereas the Palazzo guests take theirs on a roof terrace.   The route from reception to the room at the Palazzo is tortuous in the extreme and you have to go via reception because the room keys are so…
