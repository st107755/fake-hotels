In September after a weeklong cruise to the Greek Isles, we spent a few days at the Bauer IL Palazzo in Venice (the old hotel, not the modern one in front).  We went to the modern hotel by mistake and had to be ushered through the maze of buildings to the older hotel.  Both properties are owned or managed by the same people, but they have separate staffs.  In any event we didn’t enter through their front door or water taxi entrance.   We found the front desk personnel to be friendly and accommodating, yet a bit too formal for our pedestrian tastes.  In the old tradition of metal keys, each guest has to check in and out with the front desk and leave the bulky key and fob (which in this case resembled an old drapery pull) with the desk attendant who would hang it on the room…
