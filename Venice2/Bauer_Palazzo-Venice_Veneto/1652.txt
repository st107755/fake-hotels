Arriving at the Palazzo is just the first part of the very best of Venetian experience: you can either arrive by boat from the fabulous (just for once the word is justified) Grand Canal or by land down a tiny dark back alley - in Venice the street entrance was always the back door - but what a discreet way to arrive at this most sumptuous hotel in a City that almost invented the idea of "sumptuous". If you are staying in the best, that's enough: you do not need everyone else to know!     The foyer itself is small and like a jewel-box in deep,venetian colours, rich with silk and old chinese lacquer.  The chinese flavour seems odd until you remember that Marco Polo was Venetian...
