From the moment you arrive, and unless you have your own boat, the only ways available are eithers the hotel's frequent shuttle service (free transit from San Marco) or a water-taxi, you realise that you are miles away from the hustle and bustle of Venice. The hotel is luxurious without being gaudy. There are no enormous lounges but a series of cosy salons all individually decorated and some wonderful internal and external gardens. The Island of San Clemente was previously a convent and the nun's spirit of calm seems to have survived its transition into a very grand hotel. The bedrooms are spacious and decorated with meticulous good taste, the bathrooms are well equipped and roomy. Needless to say the hotel provided an excellent breakfast.
