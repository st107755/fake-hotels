My wife and I stayed eight nights at this beautiful island hotel, which has well manicured grounds, a superb swimming pool, friendly staff and great food. It really is a lovely haven of tranquility after having spent the day in Venice!  The main building is what it is, and so despite high ceilings and wide corridors it can feel enclosed, and how much better the bedrooms would be with balconies! But then again, as a former asylum and monastery, such architectural niceties were not desirable, no doubt.  The main restaurant, Acquerelle, served some of the best food I have tasted for years, and it was a pleasure to meet the Executive Chef.  There is a very convenient shuttle to and fro from San Marco, but getting anywhere else was at least €50 each time. We visited Murano, Burano…
