The Danieli is an experience per se. The main building is a museum, and very well kept; staff is kind, rooms are too many to give a general opinion, but I can say that you get what you have been said @ time of booking: if you pay for the view you get an astonishing one. Food is nice, and if you can enjoy the roof terrace it is even better (if you are lucky, even a late-november lunch can be served outside).
