There are those who love Venice and those who hate it -- if you don't like to be in the middle of tourists, stay in an 16th century palace, and like the vitality and noise that comes with it, then save your money.  However, if you want top experience Venice as it must have been at the height of its economic power, then stay at the Danieli.    We were fortunate enough to get an upgrade to a second floor suite overlooking the lagoon -- an amazing treat (Room 228).  But you don't have to stay in a suite to enjoy the Danieli.  In fact, the view from the roof top Terrace is even better and you can have an amazing breakfast there every day.
