We stayed at the Danieli for 3 nights in the old building, with silk wall coverings and beautiful wood surrounds. It's an exquisite hotel, with views from the suite/room that are breathtaking. The rooms are comfortable and well appointed, they're also looked after by an  excellent house keeping team.  The hotel staff are friendly and if you need to get by in English they are readily able to switch languages to help you, which isn't the case with some hotels elsewhere in Italy. The only downside to the Danieli is the volume of people coming and going sometimes during their stay, it's popular and with the other wings to the hotel, reception can get busy sometimes.
