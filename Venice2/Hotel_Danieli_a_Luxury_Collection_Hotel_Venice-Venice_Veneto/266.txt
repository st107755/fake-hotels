Bottom line, folks, this is a Marriott.  A good one, but identical really to the Marriott offering around Europe, at least.    However, its historic presence on the front at Venice, along from St Mark's Square make it special.  So also does breakfast on the Terrazzo Danieli overlooking the lagoon, cocktails in the piano bar and simply walking down the grand staircase taking in the 14th Century interior.    The rest? A mixed bag.  Reception, located in a corner under the stair, does not cope with the volume of 210 rooms over three buildings.  It needs to move out of there and be better organised.
