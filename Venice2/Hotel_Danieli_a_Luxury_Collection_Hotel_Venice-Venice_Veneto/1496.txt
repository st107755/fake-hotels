This is  without a doubt one of the most delightful hotel experiences one can have in Venice. It is old world coming into the new world. A beautiful blend of 16th and 17th century art, furniture, glass, and 21st century lifestyle.  Breakfast came with our room and what a breakfast it was for our family of 8. We ate on the rooftop terrace with the best view and scenery of the Grand Canal and San Sebastian, we saw cruise ships, and  gondolas and tourists and locals strolling to and from San Marcos Piazza, the Adriatic Sea in the distance, Lido and the islands. Wow, this is the place to start the day for sure. The food was delicious, well prepared with an amazing buffet and choices beyond compare. We had four rooms, one was a suite.
