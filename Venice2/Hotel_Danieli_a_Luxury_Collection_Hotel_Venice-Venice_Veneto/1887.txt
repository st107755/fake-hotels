You can approach the Danieli as a 600-year-old treasure, in which case its quirks can be excused, or you can look upon it as an extremely expensive but ostensibly modern hotel, in which case perhaps they can't. I prefer to look upon this legendary hotel as a fantastic museum piece and our stay here as a memorable event; moreover, I'm a little mystified by the quibbles with the service. Over and over during our three days here, we were greated by a procession of doormen and concierges smilingly wishing us a good morning or a good evening.
