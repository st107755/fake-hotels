The Danieli is a splendid hotel. We arrived by water taxi after a long travel day. Our room wasn't available yet so we checked our luggage and walked to a nearby restaurant for lunch. Other reviewers have mentioned the crowds. One must expect this because Venice is a popular tourist destination. We returned to the hotel after lunch and were escorted to our room which appeared to be in an annex of the main building. The room décor was lovely with a separate walk in closet and storage room. The room had two windows which were covered by drapes. We were extremely disappointed when we looked out the windows. We did not pay for a water view but our windows faced a deteriorating building and a fire escape that was draped with old paint cloths and rotting fruit that had been left on…
