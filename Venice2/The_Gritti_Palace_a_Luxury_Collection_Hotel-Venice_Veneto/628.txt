We booked a three night stay at the Gritti Palace (for my 60th birthday treat) over a year in advance. In the intervening time I read many reviews saying what a wonderful hotel it was and I had looked at all the photographs on the website – would it live up to expectations and be as good as everyone seemed to say it was? – undoubtedly yes, in fact it was better, as the pictures really do not do it justice. This beautiful 15th century palace, home to the former Doge Andrea Gritti, is in the perfect position for a stay in Venice. Not only is it in very easy walking distance of St. Mark’s Square but it occupies a prime site on the Grand Canal opposite the famous Santa Maria della Salute church. A water taxi took us directly to the hotel’s flower bedecked, private, landing stage…
