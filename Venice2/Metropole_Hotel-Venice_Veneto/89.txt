We only went to the Oriental Bar for cocktails because I had to see the space.  The Metropole was originally the orphans home called the Pieta where Antonio Vivaldi worked. The Pieta Church next door was actually built a few years after Vivaldi's death (though he may have had some input in its design). The church where Vivald's music was played in his lifetime is now the Oriantal Bar in the Metropole (though the church probably extended both upstairs and back behind the bar).  The odd door (now sealed) to the left of the bar was a revolving door where people dropped off their unwanted children (the revolving door was to keep the babies from freezing).
