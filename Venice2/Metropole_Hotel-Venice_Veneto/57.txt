We reserved the room for New Year’s Eve in Venice last minute so we didn’t expect a primo room.  What was offered was a room in the annex building with a garden view.  As we didn’t plan to spend much time in the room we figured no biggie.  Upon checking in, we inquired about any upgrade available but we were informed that the hotel was sold out. As the porter walked us to our room I noticed that we were walking through a dining area.  I assumed we were going to another corridor when we suddenly came to a door on one of the exterior walls of a large breakfast dining room.  That door was our room.  I thought to myself, I’ve NEVER stayed in a hotel that had a room situated like this, and if I knew this previous to booking, I wouldn’t have booked it.  Now, the room was very nice and…
