We stayed for 4 days during the biennale and as such the place was busy and prices more expensive than other times. I loved the hotel - the decor and art collection were fascinating, and it was certainly a classy place. The staff were very friendly and attentive. I would recommend, although I did have a few little gripes:  The air con wasn't working all over the hotel and never really got an explanation for it. It was very hot and as our room was on the front it was too noisy to open the windows at night. We were moved to a room at the back eventually but as this overlooked the restaurant garden it wasn't really any quieter.
