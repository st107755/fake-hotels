I stayed two nights in the top floor junior suite facing the lagoon.  It was a beautiful corner room with large windows and a nice bathroom/tub.  The turn down service was discreet and a nice touch.  The staff arranged a water taxi departure to the train station, which was well worth the added expense.  The check in procedure felt like a sales pitch for several expensive trips and breakfast.  Like many things in Venice, the breakfast seemed exorbitant in price and I did not try it.  The concierge posed everything as a 'wonderful opportunity' etc., which did not appeal to me.  Nearby breakfast options are geared to the nearby-disembarking ferry passengers and I found stale pastries and muddy coffee to be the norm, at twice the cost of options interior to this area.
