Comfortable world class hotel near San Marco and many other attractions. Yes, that means that many other travelers enjoy the location as well and it can get crowed and tourist traps lurk around every corner, but they can easily be ignored. At check-in, the reception staff informs you about breakfast, pricing and restaurant facilities. If you can eat a big buffet breakfast at the price for lunch, go for it, otherwise pass breakfast at the hotel and look in the neighborhood for little cafés close by to have a much cheaper breakfast. Dinner is in an elegant setting, with Michelin-stared chef, and very friendly waitstaff. Our "family" room was large, 2 king beds, good enough mattresses, comfortable temperature, beds turned in the evening. TV noise came in from other room briefly,…
