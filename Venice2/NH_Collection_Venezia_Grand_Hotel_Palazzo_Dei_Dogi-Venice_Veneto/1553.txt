It's worth staying at this hotel just for the building. It's like staying in a dolls' house. I went down the stairs from our room on the top floor each day, rather than using the lift, so I could look at the paintings and the chandeliers in the hallways on the first floor. There is a lovely garden, containing, among other things, an old ice house. My partner called this 'The chamber of consonants'. Go inside and say a few words. You will see what she means.  The small flaws were so easily fixable that I can't see why the management doesn't just sort them. The staff were very helpful and efficient with the exception of the person on reception when we checked out. She definitely needs a refresher course on dealing with customers politely.
