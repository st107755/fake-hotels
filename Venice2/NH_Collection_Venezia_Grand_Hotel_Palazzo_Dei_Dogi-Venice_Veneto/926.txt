We spent two nights in this grand dame of a hotel, formerly known as the Dei Dogi, but now operated under the competent Marriott umbrella as part of the Boscolo Autograph collection. The lobby is Venetian elegance, the doors and fixtures feel rich and vintage. The walls of the room we were in (basic room) were coated with silk fabric over padding, a treatment I have only seen in old mansions. The marble bathrooms had elegant fixtures. Service was Marriott impeccable, and breakfast was delicious and varied. Now the quirks: As others have mentioned, there is a dilemma of a hot, stuffy room in shoulder season when the A/C has been shut off for the season, and you can't open the windows for fear of being eaten by mosquitos.
