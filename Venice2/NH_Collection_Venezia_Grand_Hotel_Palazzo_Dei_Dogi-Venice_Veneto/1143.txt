This hotel is very different like many in Venice are.  It is a Palace that has been turned in to a hotel. It is furnished accordingly with an ornate look.  It's location is away from the crowded areas of Venice but if you can walk it is not a problem to get there from the San Marco, Rialto areas.  It is in a very quiet neighborhood. The staff here was very helpful with restaurant and sightseeing plans.  Daniel and Ivan were just the best.  We had a wonderful recommended lunch in Burano and 2 dinners in quaint neighborhood restaurants close to the hotel.  We did take advantage of the shuttle to San Marco and also rode the valporetto for the 4 days that we stayed there.  Breakfast was good and included in our room rate.
