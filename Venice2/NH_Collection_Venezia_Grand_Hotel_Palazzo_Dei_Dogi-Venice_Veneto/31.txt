We stayed one night at this hotel.  We took "vaporetto" route 5.2 to the hotel from the train station and, upon leaving the hotel, walked back to the train station (about a 10-minute walk), which involves crossing about four small bridges over canals.  Although the hotel is only a short walk (about 5 minutes) from some of Venice's principal commercial areas, it is in a quiet area of the city.  The hotel and its rooms are beautiful.  A quick look on the Internet suffices to learn some of the hotel's history.  The rooms are ornate and functional.  Ours overlooked  a canal below.  Our bed was comfortable; sleep quality was excellent -- no noise.  We liked the buffet breakfast.  The hotel's garden is exceptionally attractive and worth a visit.
