This is the only Venitian hotel that's owned by Marriott. We stayed 3 nights on points and were initially disappointed to be ushered to a room, not in the palatial main building, but rather, beyond the huge garden at the back of the property. Our room was cottage-like with its own quaint garden patio. If this was "the back of the bus" because we used points, we were fine with it. A huge king size bed dominated the room, accented with a rustic cathedral ceiling and Murano glass light fixtures. The bath was spa-like and spacious. What keeps this review from 5 stars was the poor performance of the shower. The water temperature swung dramatically from scalding to icy with no touch of the faucet. It was very difficult to shower.
