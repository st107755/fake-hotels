It is removed from the crowds of the great sight-seeing spots. It overlooks the sea at on end and the canal on another end. They do have a complimentary water taxi to the more busy parts of Venice (you need to book in advance).  It would have been a 5 but the size of the rooms was somewhat small for a hotel of this quality; having said that, it is possible that there were more spacious rooms since these are mostly old buildings where room sizes are not standardised. We had two meals (a lunch and a complementary breakfast) and both were very good. However, they are firm believers in the slow food movement so do factor that in if you have to catch a train/flight or a cruise ship.
