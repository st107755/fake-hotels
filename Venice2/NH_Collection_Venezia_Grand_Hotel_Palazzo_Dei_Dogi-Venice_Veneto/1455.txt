My wife and I are travelling through Europe, we booked this hotel using Marriiott points.  We enjoyed it so much, we extended our stay from 2 days to 5 days. The hotel and staff are full of old world charm. The building dates from the 1600s and was originally a palace, then a consulate and also a monastery. It has front and back entrances on adjacent canals and the public ferry service stop is close nearby. You can also walk across the bridges and go to St Mark's Square in about 20 minutes if you don't get lost, but getting lost is half of the fun in Venice where every small street brings more surprises.
