The relatively low key entrance to this former embassy hides soaring ceilings and a true Venetian experience. Located in the Cannaregio, the hotel has the largest private garden in Venice (or at least that's what we were told)!  The interior of the hotel is marble and Murano made chandeliers with seating on every level that invites you to stop and simply appreciate the paintings and detailed interior decorations. We stayed in a room 207, which featured two comfortable king sized beds on two separate floors, perfect for a family of four.  The upstairs was a loft reachable by a circular stairway that while solid, wouldn't be something you'd want to race up and down on.
