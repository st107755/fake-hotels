Mini-palazzo on the Grand Canal with just 9 rooms and hospitable boutique feel. Wonderful views from canal-side rooms but also the communal terrace where breakfast and cocktails are served, so you should still consider this hotel if you can't afford the canal-side.  There is clearly a total commitment to service, which is excellent, led from the front by the owner Matteo. When he's around he cooks the breakfasts himself. They also ran a great concierge: each night they were able to recommend a restaurant to suit our mood and secure us one of the best tables.  We stayed in one of the standard rooms at the back and then the junior suite.
