Stayed two nights. Upgrade to a ground floor room in an annex with a spectacular view of Venice (usually a benefit of my Amex). Positives - view; ability to escape the maddening crowds of Venice. Negatives - cannot escape the institutional feel of the property (use to be a hospital); limited dining options (ate at the pool restaurant - ok meal); dining prices are obscene even by high end hotel standards (to be expected at a hotel of this stature but since you are on an island you are captive); common Euro bathroom dilemma of no shower - just tub and hand spray; service - house keeping and valet - was slow or failed to show up.  On my trip I stayed at Villa San Michele in Venice and Castiglion del Bosco in Tuscany. All top end hotels. San Clemente Palace is a cut below those.
