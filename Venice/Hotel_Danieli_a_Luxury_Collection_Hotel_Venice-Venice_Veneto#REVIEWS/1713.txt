Just to frame our point of view; we are lucky enough to be well travelled and appreciate hotels that reflect a city's culture and history.  We don't need complete renovations and modern predictability to be satisfied guests.  What we do need is a sense that a hotel cares that you feel welcome and takes pride in their services; if these things matter to you, then you will want to stay elsewhere.    There are some good points to the Danieli; our check in was gracious, and our room, a junior suite with lagoon view, had comfortable beds, great linens, and a wonderful sense of history.  The evening turndown service was warm and charming, which apparently was the exception.
