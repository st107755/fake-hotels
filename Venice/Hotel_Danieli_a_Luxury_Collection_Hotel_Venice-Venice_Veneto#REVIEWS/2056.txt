You cannot fail to be impressed by the grandeur of this hotel, despite its inconspicous entrance just along from the Doge's Palace.  The hallway, the vast salons dripping in art and mirrors on each floor, the staircases and the stunning views from the terrace.  Our room (marketed as a super deluxe something or other) was in the old building and was delightfully light and Venitian, if a tad compact.  However the canal noise early the next morning led us to ask to be moved the next day which the Danieli did, and upgraded us, to a room which was actually a bit more homogenous executive style (but quiet) so that was great too.  The reception staff and the concienrge staff were friendly and helpful at all times.
