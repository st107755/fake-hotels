Stayed for 4 nights at the end of July to celebrate wife's birthday. We wanted something special and close to the main attractions so booked this hotel. The Danieli is a very famous hotel in the heart of Venice. It has retained much of its character and charm even though it must be tempting to add the odd new luxury or two. Credit to the owners for keeping its historical facade and furnishings. The hotel itself is a tourist attraction and many do visit to take pictures of the lobby and furnishings.  The location is superb and cannot be beaten, approx 5 mins walk/stroll from St Marks square.   We had room on top floor with the addition of a balcony which was great for watching the rooftops of Venice and the gondolas on the side canal below. Not many rooms have a balcony.
