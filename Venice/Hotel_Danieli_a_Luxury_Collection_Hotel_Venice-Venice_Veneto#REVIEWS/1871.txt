First, let me say that Hotel Danieli is located on the Grand Canal and is accessible by water taxi. I saw many tourists schlepping luggage through the winding paths of the city and I can only imagine what an inconvenience that was for them. The waterfront hotels are costlier, but when you consider the only "roads" in Venice are waterways, the extra money spent is worth it.  Secondly, the Danieli is literally around the corner from St. Mark's Square (Piazza San Marco), the Doge's Palace, and the Basilica of San Marco. As far as convenience, it doesn't get better than this hotel.   The hotel was gorgeous and the staff was gracious, albeit formal. You will find the doorman, etc., in coat and tie with white gloves.
