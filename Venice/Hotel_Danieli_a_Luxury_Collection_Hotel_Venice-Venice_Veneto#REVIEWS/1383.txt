I write from my room in the Danieli, with the windows wide open to the maritime bustle of the Lagoon and a cool breeze blowing in. One look out the window is enough to make you pass over many of the details of our stay uncritically. With a bit of imagination, you can believe yourself back in the Venice of a bygone era, with sailboats, gondolas, highly-polished vintage water-taxis and cargo barges charging back and forth and seemingly at each other in the endless bumper-car action that passes for navigation in these waters.  You enter just off Piazza San Marco, direct on the waterfront through an enormous revolving door and into an San Simeon type lobby in pure Venetian Gothic splendor. Surely you have now arrived.
