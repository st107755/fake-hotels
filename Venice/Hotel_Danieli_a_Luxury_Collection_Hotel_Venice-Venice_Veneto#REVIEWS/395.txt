The hotel has everything you would expect from the term luxury, but Venice itself has changed greatly since I last visited 30 years ago, and that caused our problem. We booked a room overlooking the lagoon, and were assured on arrival that the volume of passing pedestrians and vessels on the water would be very much less after about 9.30 pm. There are vaporetto stops, gondola parks, and numerous souvenir booths directly in front of the hotel, and the noise from all this plus the incredible amount of water traffic was tremendous during the day with the windows open, and still very noisy with them closed. We were advised to keep the windows closed, but not warned that the air conditioning for the entire hotel was out of action that day.
