We really wanted to stay at the Danieli, as we had stayed there many years ago and had memories of an awesome, fabulous looking place.  When we pulled up in our boat and entered the lobby--it was as gorgeous as ever.  The front desk check-in person, Kyriah , was the most helpful she could be.    We ended up with a room in the "new" wing.  We accepted that because it had two MAJOR pluses--the rooms were on the second floor overlooking the grand canal, and the rooms were connecting--which is hard to find.   Notwithstanding the garish gothic/victorian/bizarre decor, the rooms were still great--very comfortable--the bathrooms were updated and the view was what you would dream of in Venice.
