I've been to Venice a half dozen times, and have even stayed in a lagoon room at the Hotel Bauer, but The Danieli is truly a once in a lifetime Venetian experience.  Built in 1400, at the beginning of Venice's glory days, this Byzantine palazzo is remarkably well preserved.  Nothing else will give you the feeling of what it must have been like to live and be entertained back in the days of the Italian renaissance.  From the famous staircase and loggia, to the first-floor lounge, to the other secluded common rooms and hallways, your eyes feast on marble, gilded wood, and incredibly lavish ceilings.  Breakfast in the fourth floor restaurant lets you dine with a 180 degree view from Lido to San Marco.
