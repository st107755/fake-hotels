We stayed at this hotel not by choice but rather were placed in it due to a booking mix up in our first choice hotel. First things first this is three star and we were not expecting anything opulent which is just as well. The decor is dark and faded and not in some shabby chic way rather just in the shabby way both in the public areas and the bedrooms. Our room backed on to a building at the rear which turned out to house a public toilet and in the morning we were regailed by the noises of the toilet being hosed down with water being filled into buckets and the never ending noise of the hand driers. The breakfast served was not great and it matched the gentleman who served it who seemed to resent the fact that we were there at all.
