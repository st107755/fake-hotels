This former palace of an eminent patrician family turned into a luxury hotel is a true Venetian landmark. Before the renovation of the hotel two years ago, staying at the Gritti was a bit like living in a museum, all very charming, but with very visible signs of wear and tear. I always expected the old elevators to break down when going up to or down from our room. They never did, though. Now the Gritti Palace is restored to its old splendor with all you expect from a Venetian palace: Marble floors, Murano crystal chandeliers, fantastic local fabrics on the walls, and fine works of art. Most important, the Hotel should now be safe from the seasonal high tides (aqua alta) in winter. One of the innovations is a small spa and gym on the ground floor in the rear.
