The Hilton sits on Giudecca, an island just south of the main part of the city.  We arrived at the rail station (St. Lucia), from which you take a vaporetto (the Actv 2 or 4.2 lines) for a relatively short ride to Palanca.  As you are arriving at Palanca, look behind you to the right for the old brick factory buildings - that is what has been repurposed as the hotel.  You will have to walk back along the waterfront and cross two bridges (carrying your bags up steps).  Alternatively, you can stop on the other side of the hotel on the 4.2 line, and then cross only one bridge with no steps to navigate, but it's a little trickier, as you have to go inland a bit and walk through the neighborhood.
