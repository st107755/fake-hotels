This is a Hilton Hotel, so the assumption is that it isn't going to be cheap - and accordingly it isn't.  The cost of food and drinks will invariably be relative to the ability of the guest to pay, not the quality of the food and drink on offer. It's a 5* hotel, so if you can afford to stay here, it's a natural assumption that you can afford the tariff to eat here.  Neither of these two assumptions justify excessive costs. The food is made up of the same ingredients as any restaurants in Venice and the drinks are largely the same bottled spirits readily available throughout Venice. With the  purchasing power of the Hilton behind them, they'll pay less than other places for those ingredients.
