Spent three nights at the hotel (Sunday-Wednesday) in August 2015. Arrived from the airport by private boat.  Hotel is vey large, located in a historic building the was reconstructed from a flour mill. As a result the building is quite unique and is worth walking through for a while.  Rooms are beautiful and fairly spacious. Was somewhat disappointed as our canal-view room was in the middle of the hotel, and since the sides are physically closer to the canal, view was limited to whatever was in front of the window.  Breakfast was generous, but restaurant a little busy (in a room adjacent to where the food is). We has lounge access so ate at the lounge the other days and were happier with that.
