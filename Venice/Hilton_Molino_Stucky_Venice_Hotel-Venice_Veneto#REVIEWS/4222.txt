I was in Venice at the end of a Seaborne cruise in August and the idea of staying on a few days appealed to me. However, everything you have ever heard ( well, maybe not everything!) of Venice and the heat and crowds, tend to be true. The option of staying apart from the crowds yet accessible was answered by the Molino Stuckey. After you get over the rather odd name, it is a most welcoming place. I was fortunate to have a room facing the canal and the Dorsoduro quarter. There is a free launch which goes back and forth but be aware of the times as there are gaps and you are stuck( no pun intended) .....but then a stroll takes you along a delightful promenade.  The hotel pool has a view but few chairs, so it is really a spot for a fast dip which I did.
