We entered the lobby to a mad house. Seven cruise ships in port with the travelers ending in Venice staying here. We had 70 cruisers trying to get a room with the crowd. The hotel worked hard to take care of all of us. We had a room on the 3rd floor with a view of the Grand Canal. The room was very well furnished. A full bathroom with a tub my wife enjoyed. The hotel is away from the mass noise across the canal and is located near some very excellent restaurants that will blow your taste buds. The executive lounge was good. All hotels are scaling back from the old days. They did have beer and wine in the evening. The buffet breakfast was pretty wide spectrum of items. The eggs, beans, and sausage were cold. The staff was very helpful with the constant hustle.
