Originally I booked a hotel overlooking the grand canal. I had concerns about small ancient rooms and crowds everywhere so I switched to HMS. After staying in antique villas throughout Italy this was a nice change. Once an old flour mill it was then converted into a hotel. Its full of history but modern at the same time. The lobby is large. There is a bistro near the lobby that serves drinks and a limited menu (good pizza) and there are two other restaurants on the premises. One serves Italian food and the other serves Mediterranean food.  We didn't have the time to eat at the nicer restaurants so I can't comment on them.  We did have executive privileges and the breakfast was very good along with happy hour snacks. Since we were visiting in October the pool was closed.
