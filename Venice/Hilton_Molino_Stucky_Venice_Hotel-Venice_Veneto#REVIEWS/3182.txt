I love the concept of the old flour mill being converted in to a 5 star hotel and I enjoyed looking at the old photographs before it was renovated. The rooms were very large, but we had to turn off the air conditioning as it was effecting our eyes and throat. In the end we left the window open all night, which meant it was quite cold in the morning being February. The breakfast was very good, the food was located in a separate room to where you actually sit and eat. The executive lounge was unusual as I felt it was tucked away and it was a little claustrophobic with no atmosphere. I thought with the size of the building they could have situated it overlooking the canal. I did love the open top bar on the eight floor, stunning view at night.
