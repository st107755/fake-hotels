First one must realize the Molino Stucky is located on Giudecca Island. To my husband and me this was a big plus, because in August the hoards of tourists are a 5-minute boat ride away.  There were so many tourists, groups trailing along behind their flag-carrying leader, that one day we just stayed on Guidecca and walked from one end to the other and seeing how the real residents live and work.  (The Molino Stucky is at one end and the lovely Cipriani Hotel at the other.)  The first afternoon on the island I popped into a small market to buy a bottle of water, but had nothing smaller than a 20 Euro note.  The owner said, "Just take the water and pay me tomorrow."  I doubt that would happen in an establishment near St. Marks Square.
