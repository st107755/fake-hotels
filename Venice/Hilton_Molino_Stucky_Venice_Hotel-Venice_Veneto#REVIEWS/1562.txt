One of the challenges, when first visiting a city, is choosing which area to stay in, and I struggled with Venice, in this respect more than most.   I eventually opted for Giudecca, because we tend to prefer to be away from the more crowded areas of cities, particularly those with a big tourism industry. Although it's only a ten to fifteen minute ride away from St Mark's Square on the waterbus, Giudecca feels much more residential, and quite untouristy, as evidenced by local restaurants, and shops such as mini markets, bakeries, fishmongers etc.   The Hilton is easy to locate, as it dominates the western end of the island. As a hotel, it is entirely acceptable, with comfortable rooms, and helpful and friendly staff.
