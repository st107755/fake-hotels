The biggest criticism of this hotel is possibly it's greatest asset. Bear with me...  It's location on a separate island, away from the main tourist areas, means peace and quite when you need it but it also means you could be a little restricted to where you eat and drink in the evening and you'll find the hotel takes advantage of this with rather inflated prices (even for Venice). (And here I'm using London hotels as an example - something I have far more experience of but would think would be comparable).  Some examples of the hotel's attempts to fleece its customers:- 26 Euro for a very average burger and chips served in a paper basket or a completely ridiculous 40 Euro for the cheapest bottle of wine in the admittedly excellent Sky Bar (great views and great service).
