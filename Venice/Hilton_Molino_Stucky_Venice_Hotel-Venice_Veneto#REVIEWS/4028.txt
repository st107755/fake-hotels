We had reservations about staying on the Giudecca as it seems quite cut off from the main action in Venice. However any misgivings were quickly dispelled when we eventually got to our hotel (beware - the location of the shuttle boat at San Marco has changed but the website didn't tell us this!). The building is magnificent and is a  sympathetic conversion of a 19th century flour mill. The hotel is tasteful, comfortable and spacious with lots of sitting areas on the ground floor. As you'd expect there's a reasonable amount of Murano glass but there's no bling - just good style. We ate in one of the restaurants and had a good meal but it was very expensive and didn't really represent good value.
