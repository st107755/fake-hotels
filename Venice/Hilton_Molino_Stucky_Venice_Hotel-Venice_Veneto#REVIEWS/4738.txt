We stayed recently for a 3 night city break. Having booked a twin room long in advance at a heavily discounted rate, we were delighted to be told on arrival that we had been upgraded to a 5th floor canal view.  This was actually a corner room, with great views to two aspects.  It was spacious and comfortable - beds like clouds, a well-appointed bathroom and generously sized towels.  The corridor outside smelt great, like sandalwood, but not in a sickly air-freshener kind of way.  Breakfast was superb - an array of hot and cold savoury items, fruit, cereals and bakery, and of course prosecco.  A tip - coffee drinkers are best requesting a cappuccino or espresso, which are made to order, rather than just coffee which is poured from a jug and seems to be filtered.
