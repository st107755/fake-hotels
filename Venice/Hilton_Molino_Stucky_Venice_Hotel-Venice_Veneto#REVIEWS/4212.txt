The hotel, a converted flour mill and for a huge brick building has quite stunning architecture. The area around the hotel is very quiet and there are a couple of canal side restaurants and bars close by hotel but it is a bit 'industrial' as a location. We booked an executive package which gave us an upgraded room and access to the Hilton Executive Lounge (very average). The receptionist was friendly and throughout our 4 day stay all the reception and concierge staff were very helpful. Our room was roomy, quite pleasant and the A/C very quiet. When researching the hotel we read repeatedly that breakfast was a key feature of the hotel. It isn't! The food choices were basic and the staff were woeful.
