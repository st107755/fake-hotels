Hotel caters to cruise and convention groups so the lobby seems chaotic and crowded much of the time. Executive lounge offers limited drinks and food and is poorly serviced (e.g. ice buckets are often empty). Breakfast is usually slow as there is only one chef at the omelet station resulting in long line ups. The dining area is spread over a number of rooms so service is slow. We had a deluxe king room which was large and comfortable with a view of the Giudecca Canal.  Occasionally we could hear the TV from the room next door. Bottled water was not provided in rooms daily so guests were all taking large bottles from the executive lounge. The gym is in three adjoining rooms, which are complicated to get to.
