Well-trained helpful staff do not compensate for the overwhelming size of this former industrial complex.  The collection of seven late nineteenth century buildings (former flour mill, Hilton since 2007) imposes its own personality. It is very big (370+ rooms, 40+ suites) without many lifts, so there is a great deal of walking - some guests have difficulty finding their room.  The size may be an advantage for a hotel that provides the biggest conference facilities in Italy. But apart from conferences and the flow of pre- and  post-cruise passengers, the location is not good for a restful family vacation.  The architecture is gloomy, so the hotel’s décor policy should try to lighten this, in which it is not always successful.
