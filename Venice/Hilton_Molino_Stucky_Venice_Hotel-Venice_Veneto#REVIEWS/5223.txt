Travelers to Venice might quickly overlook the Molino Stucky because of its Hilton brand, but that would be a mistake. Constructed in partnership between the city of Venice and Hilton in 2007, the hotel was created in the shell of a 19th century brick flour factory after a five-year renovation project that is considered one of Italy’s finest historic reuse projects.   We were extremely impressed at the level of construction detail that we observed throughout the property, noting that Hilton spared no expense in using the finest woods, marbles and metal finishes throughout the sprawling complex.
