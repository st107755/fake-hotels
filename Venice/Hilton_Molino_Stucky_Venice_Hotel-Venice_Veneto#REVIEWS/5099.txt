Giudecca, formerly unfashionable and a boat ride from most of the sites, is a surprisingly pleasant alternative to the tourist choked main islands of Venice, and the hotel is a spectacular conversion of a splendid late 19th century flour mill. There's even a small pool on the roof wiith fabulous views.  But if cool (in the fashion not the weather sense) is what you like then you'll be disappointed by the sumptuous but bland Hilton style. There's everything you might want, in abundance, but why do you feel you might just as well be camping in the foyer of an international airport?  Friendly staff, lavish breakfast buffet, large rooms with (in our case) astonishing views, spacious bathrooms, endless amenities, but....style and soul? Absent.
