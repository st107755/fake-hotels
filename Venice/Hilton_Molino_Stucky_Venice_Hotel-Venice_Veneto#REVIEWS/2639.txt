I have many fond memories from the Molino Stucky Hilton, which is luxurious without being stuffy and central without being overrun by crowds. Plus, the fact that it is tall by Venice standards and on Giudecca rather than in the main part of Venice allows it to boast mesmerizing views of one of the world's most iconic cities.  In terms of pure opulence, the Molino Stucky Hilton cannot match the Venice's Gritti Palace for example, or the Danieli. But I always feel like I am in a museum in that kind of hotel. The Molino Stucky Hilton has a kind of livable opulence, which I much prefer.  And it's true that while Giudecca is within sight of St. Mark's Square, it takes a free hotel-operated shuttle service to get there.
