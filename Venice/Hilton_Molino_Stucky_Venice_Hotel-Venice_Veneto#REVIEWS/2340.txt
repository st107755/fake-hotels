To be quite honest I don't really like Hiltons (or other chain hotels) as they are usually quite impersonal and could be anywhere.  However the Hilton in Venice is in an old flour mill and many of the original features have been retained.  This gives it much more charm and character than other Hiltons.  The shuttle bus to Piazza San Marco is every 40 minutes from 9 am to midnight or so (but check the timetable for breaks) and takes 10-15 minutes.  It's very reliable on time-keeping and costs a flat fee of €3.50 per person per stay so ridiculously cheap.  Unlike the wine in the bar which was an eye-watering €9 for a 125 ml glass.  Fortunately we were able to use the Executive Lounge...  which is quite small and could get crowded, but was OK and had a decent breakfast.
