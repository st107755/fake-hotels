Stayed at this hotel on 1st and 2nd November. We had a great room, duel aspect with a very spacious bathroom although the view was just of other parts of the hotel, we could have paid extra for a view over to Venice. The building is a converted flour warehouse set on an island opposite Venice. There was a complementary boat running to Venice every 20mins, but only went to St Marks Square once an hour. Venice is expensive and so were the hotel restaurants, not all of them were open for our stay and in the end we chose not to eat at the hotel as one of the dining options was just eating in what felt like the lobby. We did enjoy the roof top bar, very good service, interesting cocktails and a lovely view back over to Venice.
