I've moved the rating from 1 to 3 stars. The 1 star was due to our first night's stay. We were at first pleasantly surprised by our upgrade to a Diamond level suite. It was nice, a few extra luxuries, not much to be honest. Executive Lounge access was handy although packed at happy hour and at mid afternoon (couldn't even get a seat!) We had room 334 with an adjoining room. I swear. it was as if our neighbours were in our room. I could hear every word they said, I knew their nationality, I heard them burp, I heard the rustle of their magazine/newspapers. At night I couldn't even sleep because of the man's snoring! And around 5am his mobile rang and of course he didn't pick up so we lay there wide-eyed begging for him to pick up the thing or at least hit the divert button.
