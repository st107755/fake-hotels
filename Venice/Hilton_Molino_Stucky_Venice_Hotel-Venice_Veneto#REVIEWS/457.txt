Considering what this hotel is charging per night, I had expected more. The rooms are pretty dated and could use some updates. The furniture and bathroom show the use of years and our toilet was rather smelly from urine scale. Unfortunately, our room also featured a bunch of mosquitos. I understand that this isn't really their fault since the hotel is right at 2 canals, but waking up covered in bites (although I had killed the 7 that I had been able to find before going to bed, but I guess there must have been more) is still not pleasant. They should install insect screens. Our room rate included breakfast which was pretty good. Thumbs up for the freshly squeezed orange juice, thumbs down for the dry bread and rolls.
