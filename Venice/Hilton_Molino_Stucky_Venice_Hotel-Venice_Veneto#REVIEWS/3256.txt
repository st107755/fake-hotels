We stayed here over the Christmas and New Year holidays. The hotel rooms are comfortable and clean. There was a nice view of the San Marco fireworks on New Year's Eve from the Hilton's rooftop (all guests of the hotel were welcome, though the SkyBar veranda was closed to guests who ate at Bacaromi and Aromi). The inside of SkyBar had a dj on New Year's Eve as well. There are four restaurants and a bar in this hotel. It was convenient to have all of that since the hotel is on the island of Giudecca in Venice (not on San Marco where it seems most people stay). The restaurants, however, are expensive, but convenient since once you get to Giudecca you might not feel like taking the boat out again. We ate in the Rialto lounge occasionally and found that to be very easy.
