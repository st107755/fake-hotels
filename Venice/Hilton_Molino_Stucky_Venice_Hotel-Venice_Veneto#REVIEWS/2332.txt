Due to structural constraints of the original industrial building, the windows of our Executive Room (which boasted a small sitting-room with a sofa, a chair and a second TV) are tall and narrow. They don't let in a lot of light, and when opened, are quite difficult to shut properly again without climbing on a chair and using some amount of force... Altogether impractical, to the point that the hotel discourages you to open them at all, which is a shame when in Venice, where you don't want to breathe only canned air, even if you're American!  We had not a courtyard view but a side view, which means that our windows opened on the eastern side and give us bice views of the Giudecca Island, the canal, and even Venice proper, with some neck work.
