The property has been updated very nicely - rooms are modem and well-sized with comfortable furnishings.  The staff are attentive and responsive without being intrusive, and the breakfast is probably the nicest I’ve seen in a chain hotel in Europe. The view across the Guidecca canal is amazing.   The hotel-provided shuttle to San Marco is workable, and the vaporetto stop is a brief walk, but it does mean that getting into the main area of Venice takes some more thought and planning than just walking out the door, and in November it can be damp and chilly. There were also a number of professional conferences taking place during our stay which sort of dampened the vacation vibe.   So, I think this is a hotel that’s actually better during peak season than shoulder season.
