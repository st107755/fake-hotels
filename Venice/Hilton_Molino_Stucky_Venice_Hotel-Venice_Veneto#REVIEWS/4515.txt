The Giudecca was once upon a time home to Venezia's Jewish community. It also housed palaces and gardens before turning into one of the archipelago's shipyards. Once neglected, the island now boasts hotels and is a great place for residential life. The Hilton Molino Stucky is a gem on this island. Just a short boat ride away from both the Zattere and the Piazza San Marco, the former flour mill gleams at night, and the nicely-lit "Hilton" sign is recognizable from across the water.  I traveled here with friends who had a series of business meetings at the hotel, and I spent my time traveling back and forth between the Giudecca and the Piazza San Marco.
