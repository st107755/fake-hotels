This is a very nice hotel - just not extra special.  It is located across a canal from "Venice" proper but there is a frequent and convenient shuttle that goes to St. Marks square regularly so not much of an inconvenience.  This is a big hotel and feels like it.  The rooms are very nice and well appointed.  We had a suite overlooking the canal.  The front desk was especially friendly and helpful.  The hotel connects several historical buildings and it does not feel like an impersonal modern hotel.  The "executive lounge" was a very narrow glass walled off area in the main lobby that wasn't especially nice.  The breakfast buffet is ok but the food is in one room access only by a narrow hall and hordes of people trying to go through in both directions.
