This delightful hotel is contained within part of a former flour mill building (like Harrods repository in London, only nicer and far, far bigger). The public areas are really spacious but the Executive Lounge is little more than a corridor cafe squeezed onto the edge of a restaurant. It’s nice, overlooks the canal but there is no room for armchairs. Actually, that didn't matter as there were so many armchairs elsewhere. [But please, hotel, remove the signs in the lift and elsewhere which suggest that the Executive Lounge is on the 6th floor.]  Our Executive bedroom was small – no space for a second armchair or a second luggage rack – but the size of the rooms is dictated by the position of the huge supporting columns.
