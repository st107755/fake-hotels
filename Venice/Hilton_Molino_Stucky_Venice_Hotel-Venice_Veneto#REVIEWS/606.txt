We stayed here as part of a 'Viking' pre cruise package.  An old flour mill, the building was impressive.  However our 'standard' room was dark and gloomy, only one bedside table lamp and a small window looking onto a brick wall  opposite, it was quite depressing. The outside restaurant had a beautiful view over the canal and the food was excellent, wine was pricey.  Breakfast was fine the first day but on our last morning the service was shambolic.  Viking guests were shown to an adjacent room to the regular breakfast room where the level of service was  poor.  Tables were not cleared, a long wait for coffee because there were not enough staff for the number of people taking their breakfast.
