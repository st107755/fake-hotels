All the staff smile, the rooms have “traditional” furnishings, much like elderly Americans might have in their own homes. Thoroughly boring.  The wi-fi is charged. Ludicrous when you’re paying €300 a night for a, pretty bland, but as I said comfortable, room. Unless, that is, you sign-up to the H.Honours scheme that unleashes a torrent of email spam. That gets you slow wi-fi for "free". Something with a speed like you may be used to at home is still charged extra.  We enjoyed our stay. We took a tongue-in-cheek attitude to the hotel and laughed at its corporate cheesiness, eating at local independent restaurants and looking at small, lower start rated, independent hotels thinking “that looks lovely, we’ll stay there next time”.
