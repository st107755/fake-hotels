The Molino Stucky is an interesting spot -- it's in a converted mill/factory so it's a large property (360+ rooms) but it feels like a unique property all the same.  Lots of exposed wood ceilings and old building touches, but a relatively modern room and amenities experience as the property was converted in the late '00s.  We were in a junior suite for a family of four and the rooms were spacious (for Europe) and comfortable, with a view of the waters between the hotel island (south of Venice proper) and Venice.  We enjoyed the never-ending parade of boats, taxis, and the occasional cruise ship.  We took a vaporetto direct to the property, and they pull up direct to the quayside and porters grab your luggage (and you) off the boat.
