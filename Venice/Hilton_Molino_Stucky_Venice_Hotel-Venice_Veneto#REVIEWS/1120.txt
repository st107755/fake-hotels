Venetian hotels can be small and cramped but Hilton have managed to avoid all that with this spaciously converted old industrial unit on Giudecca.  Interior design is personal I know but but I find the interiors unimaginative but well finished in quality materials and certainly well considered: the designer obviously followed Hilton's technical services team's needs; every guest comfort is provided and catered for and the hotel runs like a well-oiled machine.  As a converted factory the architects were obviously restricted by the site conditions and one factor this forces on the hotel is the rabbit warren-like nature of wayfinding around the building. Never has finding our guestroom been such a challenge.
