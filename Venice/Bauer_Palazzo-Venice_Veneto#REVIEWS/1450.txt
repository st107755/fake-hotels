This hotel was booked without doing any proper research and largely based off its proximity to St Mark’s Square. We definitely lucked out with this hotel as it has the most amazing terrace on the side of the canal where breakfast is served each morning. You can also watch night settle in with a relaxing drink on the bar section of the terrace.   We only ate at the bar for which the food was ok. I would expect slightly more for a 5 star hotel but you’re better off heading into Venice itself to eat and soak up the ambiance.   A bit of advice – they hold a festival on the 3rd weekend in July each year to celebrate the passing of the plague. This dates back from the 1570’s and all the boats congregate in the lagoon to watch a huge firework display.
