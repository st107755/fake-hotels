My husband and I stayed here for 4 nights at the start of our honeymoon in April 2011, and asked for a quiet room with a view of the Grand Canal - a tall order given the hectic nature of the Grand Canal, but they managed to fulfill!  As we had advised in advance that this was our honeymoon, the manager kindly upgraded us to a suite, with a lounge area, large bedroom, impressive marble bathroom, walk in wardrobe and little balcony overlooking the Grand Canal - so lovely to wake each morning to the sound of the gondaliers singing!  The hotel is made up of several buildings, and so finding your room can seem like a bit of a challenge, but take the time to enjoy the architechture and the old and new interiors and it becomes an adventure.
