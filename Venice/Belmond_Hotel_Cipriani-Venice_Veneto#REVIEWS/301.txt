Venice in summer is almost unbearable. Huge cruise liners dwarf the historic canal side buildings and disgorge thousands of passengers to join the throng of tourists of every nationality who already overflow the Piazza San Marco. Every nearby lane and bridge is blocked by a kind of human traffic jam.  What a relief it is to retreat to the calm of the Cipriani on the island of Giudecca. This elegant hotel, now part of Sea Containers' Belmond group, is decorated and furnished in a rich traditional style, with no shortage of marble where appropriate. There are a number of restaurants - which produce good rather than great food - and several bars awash with Bellinis.
