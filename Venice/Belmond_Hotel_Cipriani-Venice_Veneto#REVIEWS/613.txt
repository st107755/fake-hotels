I found this hotel to be a real grower. When we arrived I was slightly put off by the decor, which is definitely old fashioned, but over time I appreciated that as part of the overall charm. Rooms were small but definitely worth the high price tag. Set on its own island, even on Venice standards, it's an extremely picturesque setting and it feels like an oasis of clam away from the hustle and bustle. The pool area is beautiful and it's nice to mix in an afternoon lounging in the sun around all the other activities on offer in the city. The lunch was amazing. Very casual but simple and delicious food in a gorgeous setting on a terrace overlooking the water - one of the highlights of the trip for us.
