It is stating the obvious that The Cipriani is old school - the chintz is turned up to 11!  What makes it special is the location and the staff.  The location feels adventurous as the boat is needed to go into town. The island of Giudecca feels like Italy, especially on a quiet and very wet Easter weekend - old ladies nattering, people meeting for coffee, young families etc. Very different to San Marco and central Venice, which isn't particuarly nice in my view. You could also cocoon yourself at The Cipriani (and in summer I would imagine this is extremely tempting).  The staff are uniformly excellent - highly professional, charming and friendly. Probably the best I've come across.
