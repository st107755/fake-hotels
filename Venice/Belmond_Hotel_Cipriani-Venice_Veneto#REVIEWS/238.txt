Notwithstanding i have seen a run down Venice suffering from a hard national financial crisis, the Cipriani remains a splendid corner where to hide from crowds and noise. The central building has been renovated and the rooms are classy and spacious. The service remains unbeatable, but it comes at a price: it is not a place for the weak, not for travelling with a budget in mind (for instance mainland parking the car for two nights with private transport to and from the hotel for 2 guests was 480 euros).  Not surprisingly, there are virtually no Italian guests around (to the point that at the hotel restaurant, Oro, there is no Italian menu available!) and we were classified "a rarity" by a member of the staff.
