After previous visits to the Cipriani for dinner when in Venice, last months was not the special occasion we remembered. Food very much the same as everywhere else, staff similar but of-course prices are much higher. When ordering cheese at the end of the meal, six small pieces arrived one of which you could see through. As a portion of cheese costs thirty euros, we asked for more of the gorganzola. When the bill arrived, a charge of ninety euros (about £80) for cheese was on the bill - this was rectified but not professionally. Past experiences were better with exceptional personalities (I remember one called Paolo) when even the pianist helped to make the evening memorable.
