Having only stayed at the Hotel Cipriani on the Giudecca before this visit--this Marriott was the Americanized version of Venice.  It was clean, service-intensive, and predictable.  Our favorite aspect was the location: its own island, Isola delle Rose.  The island is quiet and lush.  The resort has beautiful grounds.  There are two roof terraces to walk about on, one wing has the casual restaurant and pool with bar.  All public spaces are well-thought out. Upholstery and furnishings are noticeably fine and very comfortable for sitting in solitude or gathering with others.  The children's play area is well supervised and full of fun activities.  The launch to Piazza San Marco is a treat!
