We booked a long weekend in the Dei Dogi in February 2005, having stayed in a Boscolo hotel in Lyon, where the accommodation was first class and the rate reasonable. We were attracted by the description of the hotel - in a converted palace with period decoration. Essentially, this is true. The Dei Dogi is indeed an impressive canal-side building with relatively large public spaces. It is also very convenient when arriving by water taxi from the airport. I agree with your other reviewers, that this hotel is a little distant from the centre of Venice, but this could be seen as an attraction. In any case, the canal-side walk to San Marco is no longer than fifteen minutes.  The rooms were adequate but certainly not generous for the rate charged.
