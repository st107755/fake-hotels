This is a gorgeous renovated palace. Typical Vebetian artwork is every where, classic Murano chandeliers, and guilded furniture. Unfortunately, the staffing was inadequate for the volume of guests coming and going, which translated into less than the service one might expect for a highly rated hotel. Breakfast was enjoyable. We otherwise ate outside the hotel, as we had full days in the city.  The grounds and garden were beautiful.  The bar was not a good relaxing place at the end of the day, and the barmenu less than good.  The room was beautiful. The bathroom was cramped, likely due to the age of the structure. We kept hitting our heads on the sloped ceiling over the tub. We also had brief interruptions in hot water during showers.
