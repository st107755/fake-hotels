Having stayed at the Boscolo hotels in Prague and Budapest, we were looking forward to staying at the hotel in Venice and we were not disappointed.   A welcoming smile each day and knowledgeable staff who could answer any question, no matter how obscure, about how to reach the various places we wanted to visit, made our visit to Venice easy and enjoyable.  Originally built in the 16th century, the hotel had previously been a monastery and the embassies of France and Savoy and has been beautifully restored with the decorative features you would expect in a historic Venetian palazzi including stunning Murano glass candelabra in the reception area and the first floor of the main building.
