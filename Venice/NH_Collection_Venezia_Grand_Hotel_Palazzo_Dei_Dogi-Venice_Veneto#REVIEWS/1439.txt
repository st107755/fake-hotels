If you are open to other cultures and an island that is a gorgeous mess, you will adore this hotel. We stayed five nights celebrating our anniversary and fell in love with the Boscolo.  For the best experience, visit during the fall or spring. And please don't complain about the flying insects, heat, humidity, crowds, smelly canals, and high prices. Everything is expensive in Venice because demand is so high and everything must be imported to the island. We felt like royalty riding their lovely motorboat to San Marco, and appreciated the quietness of this neighborhood far from the crowds.  Breakfasts in the garden are too kill for and definitely worth 30 or so Euros. The service and food were outstanding, and we lingered for over an hour each morning.
